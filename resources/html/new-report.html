  • Fire Risk (0-100) quantifies a property's long term average fire return interval based both on landscape-scale historical rates of observed burning and the environmental factors that drive fire activity. Lower values indicate less risk of ignition.

  • Fire Hazard (0-100) incorporates fine-scale factors used in fire behavior modeling, such as fuel amount and type, slope steepness, and canopy cover. Local extreme weather conditions are then used to predict the most dangerous fire sizes and flame lengths associated with the property. Lower values indicate less intense fires.

  • Fire Weather (0-100) captures the significant impacts that wind speed and direction (along with air temperature and relative humidity) have on the ultimate size and severity of a fire event. The fastest fire spread rates and the greatest losses are associated with widely known but difficult to map fire weather corridors. The latest fine-scale wind models reveal which locations are most threatened versus those that are relatively sheltered. Lower values indicate less threat.
